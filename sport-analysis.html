Sport-by-Sport Analysis

Deep dive into scheduling dynamics, wave viability, and revenue potential for each major youth travel sport

Executive Summary

Different sports have fundamentally different scheduling dynamics. Game length determines wave viability, which determines how many teams a TD can serve per field, which determines revenue potential. Understanding these constraints is essential for product design.

RoadGame Fit Analysis

  • Wave viability: Excellent. 70-min slots allow 2 complete waves per day with buffer. Younger age groups (40-min games) could support 3 waves.

  • Recommendation: Ideal V1 sport. Large market, predictable timing, wave-friendly, moderate weather risk.

RoadGame Fit Analysis

  • Wave concept doesn't apply. Swimming is individual events, not team pools. Can't cluster "teams" because there aren't teams playing each other.
  • The pain point exists: Families spend 6+ hours at pool for 20 minutes of actual swimming. Siblings are bored. Parents frustrated.
  • Different opportunity: Heat sheets are published in advance. RoadGame could help families identify gaps and book activities during wait times.
  • Sibling solution: Could coordinate activities specifically for non-swimming siblings during long meets.
  • Product pivot required: Not schedule optimization (can't change meet schedule), but schedule awareness + activity booking.
  • Recommendation: Different product model needed. Core thesis doesn't apply, but adjacent opportunity exists.

Key Insight: RoadGame Works Across Sports

With tournament time limits, mercy rules, and inning caps, all major team sports can support the RoadGame model. The difference is capacityhow many waves per day each sport can support.

Tier 1 (3+ waves): Basketball, Soccer, Flag Football, Lacrosse highest TD revenue upside
Tier 2 (1-2 waves): Volleyball, Ice Hockey, Softball, Baseball viable with time limits
Tier 3 (Different model): Swimming individual events, not team pools

The product works across sports. Weather risk (outdoor sports) is the main differentiator, not game length or predictability.
