## V3 Hypotheses (Disruption Thesis - NOW VALIDATED)

### H3.1: Families have agency in tournament selection
**Status:** ⚠️ Nuanced (but workable)

**Finding:** Coaches/clubs make direct decisions, BUT:
- Families choose which teams to join
- Tournament access is now a **recruitment differentiator**
- Teams market Cooperstown, Disney access to attract families
- Families can vote with feet at team selection time

**Evidence:**
> "Tournament schedules have become central to youth travel team marketing."

> "BPA Baseball's tryout page prominently displays '6X Cooperstown Champions' as a major selling point."

**Implication:** Families have indirect agency. Target clubs that use tournament quality as recruitment advantage.

---

### H3.2: Families would pay more for family-first tournaments
**Status:** ✅ VALIDATED

**Finding:** Families pay 200-300% premiums for predictable, vacation-integrated experiences.

**Evidence:**
- Cooperstown: **$10,000/team** (vs. $400-700 standard)
- Ripken: **$3,000+/week** for destination experience
- i9 Sports: **$100M+ exit** built on same-day convenience
- Scheduling chaos is parents' **#1 complaint**
- 46% increase in youth sports spending since 2019

**Key Quote:**
> "Parent willingness to pay $10,000 per team for Cooperstown's organized, predictable experience proves the model."

---

### H3.3: Clubs would participate despite coaching inconvenience
**Status:** ✅ VALIDATED

**Finding:** Coach shortage is NOT universal. Three segments have dedicated coaching:

| Segment | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| Premium clubs (ECNL, MLS NEXT) | Mandate dedicated coaches; market it as differentiator |
| Volleyball (all levels) | Built-in dual-coach model; wave scheduling universal |
| Small volunteer clubs | Natural 1:1 ratio; parent coaches commit to one team |

**Key Quote:**
> "Premium clubs, volleyball programs, and small volunteer organizations already operate with dedicated coaching that doesn't require multi-team schedule accommodations."

---

### H3.4: The economics work for a new tournament operator
**Status:** ✅ VALIDATED

**Finding:** Family-first model requires different economics but is proven:

| Traditional | Family-First |
|-------------|--------------|
| $400-700/team | $1,500-3,000/team |
| Weekend (2-3 nights) | Week-long (5-6 nights) |
| Stay-to-play rebates | Premium registration |
| 8-10 games/field/day | 4-5 games/field/day |

**Proof Points:**
- Cooperstown: **$116M** valuation (80% stake sale)
- Ripken: Expanding to 4 locations, 350K guests/year
- Surf Cup: **$120M** annual economic impact

**Key insight:** Abandoning stay-to-play becomes a competitive differentiator.

---

### H3.5: No one has properly tried this yet
**Status:** ❌ INVALIDATED (but this is GOOD news)

**Finding:** Family-first tournaments ALREADY exist and thrive at scale.

**Evidence:**
| Operator | Scale | Proof |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Cooperstown | 10,000+ players/year | $116M valuation |
| Ripken Experience | 350,000+ guests/year | Expanding, no stay-to-play |
| Surf Cup | $120M economic impact | 1 game/day, elite soccer |
| Youth Baseball Nationals | Growing | Guaranteed day off + theme parks |

**Key Quote:**
> "No documented cases of wave scheduling being tried and abandoned in outdoor sports were found."

**Implication:** The opportunity is to apply/expand the proven model, not invent something new.

---

### H3.6: Family-first can be a competitive advantage
**Status:** ✅ VALIDATED

**Finding:** Multiple industries prove scheduling as differentiator:

**Dance/Cheer:**
- RADIX: No Mini/Junior after 9pm (explicit marketing)
- EnerGy: "We run on time, even ahead of schedule"
- Studios choose competitions based on scheduling reliability

**Youth Sports:**
- i9 Sports: $100M+ exit, built entirely on convenience
- Upward Sports: 7M+ participants, "protects families from hectic schedules"

**Key Quote:**
> "Companies explicitly marketing on-time events, reasonable hours, and family experience are winning loyalty."
