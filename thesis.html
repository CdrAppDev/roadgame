Step 1

The Thesis

This is what we originally believed. The opportunity we saw, the problem we thought we were solving, and the solution we imagined.

The Observation

Youth sports families spend 10-15 weekends per year at travel tournaments. A typical weekend costs $1,200-1,500 (registration, hotel, food, gas). That's $15,000-20,000 per year, per family.

But here's the thing: most of that time is wasted. Families sit at the field for 10+ hours, watching 3 games that total maybe 3 hours of action. The rest is waiting.

The gap: Families have 6+ hours of "free time" during tournament days, but it's scattered and unpredictable—so they can't use it.

The Original Thesis

If we could cluster each team's games into a predictable block, families would have guaranteed free time. If we connected them to local activities during that time, we could monetize that free time.

The Two-Part Solution

Part 1: Clustered Scheduling

  • Instead of games scattered across 10 hours (8am, 2pm, 6pm), cluster them into a single block (8am, 10am, 12pm)
  • Everyone finishes by early afternoon
  • The entire evening is guaranteed free

Part 2: Activity Marketplace

  • Connect families to local restaurants, activities, and attractions
  • Enable group booking (teams of 15-20 families)
  • Monetize through booking commissions, premium features, or vendor access

The Value Created

Families get a dramatically better experience: predictable schedules, team bonding activities, and tournament weekends that feel like actual trips—not just waiting at fields.

The business captures value by facilitating the connection between families with free time and vendors who want their business.

The core belief: The free time already exists. It's just scattered. We cluster it—and monetize it.
