Step 4

Strategic Options

Research has validated the disruption opportunity. Now we need to decide how to execute. Here are three strategic options.

A

Create Family-First Tournaments

Recommended
Core thesis: The status quo exists because there's no family-first alternativeâ€”not because families prefer it. Create the option and capture market share.

Why This Is Validated

Cooperstown ($116M valuation), Ripken (350K+ guests), Surf Cup ($120M impact). Family-first tournaments work at scale. Premium clubs and volleyball have dedicated coaching. Families pay 2-3x premiums.

What we'd build

Destination "tourn-acation" tournaments with:

  • Genuine vacation destinations (beach, mountains, theme parks)
  • Week-long format with guaranteed free time
  • Wave scheduling (predictable game windows)
  • No stay-to-play (families choose lodging)
  • Premium pricing ($1,500-3,000/team)

Target segments

  • Premium clubs (ECNL, MLS NEXT): Already have dedicated coaches
  • Volleyball: Wave scheduling is already universal
  • Small volunteer clubs: Natural 1:1 coach ratios
Next steps: Identify underserved destination markets. Talk to 5-10 premium clubs about interest. Explore partnership vs build options.
B

Group Booking Platform

Core thesis: Accept that schedules will remain chaotic. Help team moms book group activities despite unpredictability.

What we'd build

A group booking platform for tournament team activities:

  • Find group-friendly venues near tournaments
  • Last-minute availability ("What's open NOW for 30?")
  • Team coordination tools
  • Activity providers pay for leads

Risks

  • Research shows families want predictable time, not just help coping
  • Smaller opportunity than tournament creation
  • Marketplace cold-start problem
To validate: Would activity providers pay for leads? Would team moms use a centralized tool? Can venues offer tournament-friendly flexibility?
C

Hybrid Approach

Core thesis: Start with group booking (faster to validate), then expand to tournament partnerships or creation.

Sequencing

  • Phase 1: Launch group booking platform (3-6 months)
  • Phase 2: Partner with existing operators who want family-first model
  • Phase 3: Create owned tournaments in underserved markets

Pros

  • Lower initial capital requirement
  • Build relationships with teams before tournament creation
  • Learn market dynamics before bigger bet

Cons

  • May slow down capture of larger opportunity
  • Group booking may not be compelling enough standalone
To validate: Can group booking generate enough value to justify Phase 1 investment? Are there operators interested in partnership?

Based on the research, Option A (Create Family-First Tournaments) represents the biggest validated opportunity. The model is proven. The question is execution.
