The RoadGame Solution

Clustered Scheduling: Same Games, Different Organization

RoadGame doesn't reduce games or leave fields empty. It reorganizes who plays when so each team's games are clustered together.

Compliance Reality: Most governing bodies (USSF, USA Basketball, etc.) don't mandate minimum rest between same-day games‚Äîjust changeover time. Tournaments are "compliant" scheduling back-to-back.

Health Reality: The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends rest periods to reduce injuries. RoadGame's format naturally provides this‚Äînot because rules require it, but because it's healthier.

Why Hasn't This Been Done Before?

If clustering games is so straightforward, why isn't everyone already doing it?

1. Software optimizes for the wrong thing

Tournament software (TeamSnap, Fastbreak AI) optimizes for field efficiency, not family experience. "Cluster games per team" isn't even a parameter.

2. TDs have no financial incentive

Tournament Directors get paid to fill fields, not make families happy. Clustered games = extra work with no payoff. So they say no.

3. No marketplace on the other end

Even with clustered games, families don't know what to DO with free time. You need both: predictable time + easy booking.

4. "That's just how it's done"

Tournament formats haven't changed in 30+ years. Scattered games are seen as inevitable, not a solvable problem.

5. Families don't know to ask

Parents complain about wasted time, but assume that's just how tournaments work. They don't know an alternative exists.

6. The connection was never made

"Scheduling" and "family experience" are treated as separate problems. Nobody realized scheduling IS the lever that unlocks experience.
