RoadGame V2 Strategy

Create family-first destination tournaments that compete with the status quo. The model is proven. The opportunity is validated.

The Opportunity

The status quo exists because there's no family-first alternative—not because families prefer it. Traditional tournaments optimize for field utilization and hotel rebates. Families are treated as captive audiences, not vacation customers.

The opportunity: Create destination tournaments that put families first. Compete with the status quo by offering something better—and charge premium pricing for it.

Proof It Works

This isn't theoretical. Family-first destination tournaments already work at scale—and command premium pricing.

Research Validation

No documented failures. Volleyball universally adopted wave scheduling with no reversions. No documented cases of family-first tournaments failing because of the model. The barrier is economic structure preference, not proven failure.

The Model

Create "tourn-acation" tournaments that families choose because the experience is worth the trip.

1
Destination appeal

Beach, mountains, theme parks—places families would vacation anyway. The tournament is the organizing principle for travel.

2
Week-long format

5-6 nights instead of 2-3. More hotel nights, more family time, more value for the trip.

3
Wave scheduling

AM/PM blocks like volleyball. Teams know their windows weeks in advance. Predictable free time, guaranteed.

4
No stay-to-play

"This is your family vacation—stay where you're comfortable." Differentiator that builds trust.

5
Premium pricing

$1,500-3,000/team (vs $400-700 standard). Families pay for the experience. Revenue from registration, not hotel kickbacks.

The Economics

Traditional tournaments maximize field utilization. Family-first tournaments maximize per-family value and willingness to pay.

Target Segments

We don't need to change under-staffed clubs. Three segments already have dedicated coaching and would prefer wave scheduling:

Premium Clubs (ECNL, MLS NEXT)

Already mandate dedicated coaches per team. Would prefer wave scheduling. Use tournament quality as a recruitment differentiator.

Volleyball (All Levels)

Built-in dual-coach model. Wave scheduling already universal. Proven that the model works with no reversions.

Small Volunteer Clubs

Natural 1:1 parent-coach ratios. Don't need accommodation scheduling. Often priced out of premium tournaments.

Key insight: We're not trying to change existing tournaments. TDs won't adopt wave scheduling because they need to accommodate under-staffed clubs. We're creating a new option for families who want something better.

Why This Works When Others Haven't Changed

Traditional TDs won't change because:

  • Large clubs send 15 teams with only 6 coaches—staggered schedules are intentional accommodation
  • Hotel rebates (10-20%) are a significant revenue stream
  • Field utilization metrics drive their economics

We're not asking them to change. We're creating a competing option that serves families who want a better experience—and are willing to pay for it.

Adjacent Market Proof

Dance and cheer competitions now compete on scheduling reliability. "Runs on time" is a loyalty driver. Studios explicitly choose competitions based on predictability. The same dynamic applies to youth sports—when given the option, families choose better experiences.

Next Steps

To move forward, we need:

  1. Identify underserved destination markets. Where is there unmet demand? Which sports/regions lack family-first options?
  2. Talk to 5-10 premium clubs. Validate interest in family-first tournaments. Understand what would make them register.
  3. Explore build vs partner. Create tournaments from scratch, or partner with existing operators who want to adopt the model?
  4. Identify destination + facility. Beach, mountains, or entertainment hub? Owned facility or partnership?

The opportunity is validated. Family-first tournaments work at scale. The question isn't whether the model works—it's whether we're the team to execute it.
