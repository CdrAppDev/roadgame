Step 5

The Proposal

Based on extensive research, here's a concrete proposal for what we should build. The disruption opportunity is validated. Now we need to execute.

V2 Proposal

Create family-first "tourn-acation" tournaments

Build destination tournaments that put families first: predictable scheduling, guaranteed free time, vacation-integrated experiences. Compete with the status quo by offering something better.

Why This Is Validated

Question Answer Evidence
Would families pay? YES 2-3x premiums (Cooperstown $10K/team)
Would clubs participate? YES Premium clubs, volleyball, small clubs have dedicated coaches
Do economics work? YES Cooperstown $116M valuation, Ripken expanding
Has anyone failed? NO No documented failures; volleyball adopted universally

Proof Points

Cooperstown: Sold 80% stake for $116 million. Week-long destination baseball tournaments.

Ripken Experience: 350,000+ guests/year. Expanding to 4 locations. Explicitly markets "no stay-to-play."

Surf Cup: $120M annual economic impact. 1 game/day format for elite soccer.

i9 Sports: $100M+ exit built entirely on family-first convenience.

The Model

1
Destination appeal

Beach, mountains, theme parks—places families would vacation anyway. The tournament is the organizing principle for travel.

2
Week-long format

5-6 nights instead of 2-3. More hotel nights, more family time, more value for the trip.

3
Wave scheduling

AM/PM blocks like volleyball. Teams know their windows weeks in advance. Predictable free time.

4
No stay-to-play

"This is your family vacation—stay where you're comfortable." Differentiator that builds trust.

5
Premium pricing

$1,500-3,000/team (vs $400-700 standard). Families pay for the experience. Revenue from registration, not hotel kickbacks.

Why This Works When Others Haven't Changed

We're not trying to change existing tournaments. TDs won't adopt wave scheduling because they need to accommodate under-staffed clubs. We're creating a new option for families who want something better—and targeting clubs that already have dedicated coaching.

Target Segments

Premium Clubs (ECNL, MLS NEXT)

Already mandate dedicated coaches per team. Would PREFER wave scheduling. Use tournament quality as a recruitment differentiator.

Volleyball (All Levels)

Built-in dual-coach model. Wave scheduling already universal. Proven that the model works with no reversions.

Small Volunteer Clubs

Natural 1:1 parent-coach ratios. Don't need accommodation scheduling. Often priced out of premium tournaments.

Next Steps

To move forward, we need:

1.

Identify underserved destination markets. Where is there unmet demand? Which sports/regions lack family-first options?

2.

Talk to 5-10 premium clubs. Validate interest in family-first tournaments. Understand what would make them register.

3.

Explore build vs partner. Create tournaments from scratch, or partner with existing operators who want to adopt the model?

4.

Identify destination + facility. Beach, mountains, or entertainment hub? Owned facility or partnership?
